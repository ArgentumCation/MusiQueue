Topic Proposal

by Daniel Lin

Problem Description

Parties and other events often play music. An issue is that listeners rarely have control over what type of music is played; they have little participation in the listening experience.

For example, at a party, a host may be playing a playlist of tracks, but their guests want to queue songs that are not on the playlist. In this case, the host would need to manually queue music on their phone. The broader issue is that there is no way for a host to gauge the overall sentiment of their listeners. Listeners may want to skip the current song, change the genre, or pick a song with faster beats per minute. Also, there is no way to measure the consensus of the listeners. Does the entire party hate the music that the host is playing? Or is it simply one elitist listener who really wants to skip songs?

The information problem is that there is much ambiguity between what music hosts think their guests will enjoy, and what music guests truly enjoy. Querying guests directly is often slow, inaccurate, and awkward. There are currently no digital solutions which allow hosts to tailor their music according to a holistic assessment of guestsâ€™ music attitudes.

App Description

The users of the application are guests in a space where Spotify music is played, in addition to hosts who play Spotify for their guests.

Users will use the application to queue music and vote on changes to the current queue. That is, users have the ability to see the entire music queue. They may search up music and add it to the queue. Any songs in queue deemed too unpopular may be voted on to be removed from queue, or simply removed by the host. Queuing can be rate limited.

This app will help solve the problem by abstracting and automating the process of queuing songs. This web app leverages the ubiquity of internet-connected devices to reduce the work on the host's part. Such abstraction has the added benefit of anonymizing the guests' preferences.

App Functionality

  1. Host clicks on a button to create a party, revealing a code.
  2. Guests click on a button to join a party using a code.
  3. Host begins playing music on Spotify like normal.
  4. Guests search for music and tap on results to add song to queue.
  5. Occasionally a pop-up may ask all users if they want to skip the current track or genre.
